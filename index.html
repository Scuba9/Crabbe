Crabbe Review

Franklin Crabbe is a 17 years old boy who is alcoholic and a little bit depressive. He hates his entire life and decided to go live by himself in the forest.

I think that this is a great school / project book. It is easy to read and simple.

I enjoyed the fact that there is some suspense and action.

I would definitely recommend this book to any one that loves survival stories.

The reason is because this book talks about survival, it is easy to read, it is interesting to read and there is suspense.

Letter from Crabbe to his family

Dear mom and dad,
Im writing you this letter to tell you how frustrated I am with my life right now.
I have decided to take refuge in the forest for a while so I can think about my future and what I need to do.
I feel stressed about you and dads idea that Im going to go to university, but I dont even know what I want to do. Nothing interests me right now. I just need time to think about my future.
I dont want you to worry. I have everything that I need.
Im not sure how long I will be here, but one thing for sure, I will keep you posted about how things are going.
Please, do not send in the police to look for me because they wont find me!
When I decide to come back home, I should know exactly what I want to do.
I love you both, but I need time alone right now.
Franklin,

Here is another ending

This ending starts where Crabbe and Mary were about to go raid the camp.
Mary and I were getting ready to go raid the hunters camp. We were walking through the forest to get to the camp and there was a ravine ahead of us. It was raining a lot these past few days, and the ground was muddy and slippery. Just at the moment that Mary told me to be careful, I slip down the ravine. I realize that my leg was badly injured. Mary tries hard to help me back up. She makes a splint from some sticks and my belt to help me walk. It was hard getting back up the ravine and we slipped several times. We changed our plans of raiding the camp and we return to our camp. Back at the camp, after a difficult walk we sat down and got some rest. The next day, we discuss our plans about staying or going back to civilisation. The both of us agreed that because of my leg and that we had frostbite, we had no choice but to go back for medical help. We pack only the essential supplies that we need to travel for 2 days and leave the rest there. During our walk, we plan on how and what we will explain to everyone upon our arrival. We arrive at an old logging road and someone gives us a ride to town. Mary left her way and I went straight to the hospital. She gave me her number to call if I wanted to talk. Three days later, my parents arrive, and no questions were asked. Now I feel different in a way that will help me to face challenges and to plan my future. I wasnt able to reach Mary for moral support, but just thinking about her makes me feel better.
